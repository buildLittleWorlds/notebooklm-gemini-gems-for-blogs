This guide teaches you how to use NotebookLM and Gemini Gems to research and write peer-response blog posts for ENGL 170.

What You'll Learn

Every post you write in this course responds to other writers in the network. That means you need to:

  • Understand your peer's argument — summarize it fairly before you respond
  • Find supporting research — claims need evidence
  • Draft and revise — turn your ideas into polished prose
  • Verify everything — AI can help, but you own the accuracy

Two Google AI tools can help with this workflow: NotebookLM and Gemini Gems. They work differently, and this guide will show you when to use each.

Two Tools, Two Strengths

Tool Best For How It Works
NotebookLM Source-grounded research Add sources (peer posts, articles, PDFs) to a notebook. Ask questions. Answers cite your sources.
Gemini Gems Repeatable drafting workflows Create a custom AI assistant with standing instructions. Same process every time.

The Bridge: Gemini can now attach NotebookLM notebooks as sources. This means you can combine grounded research (NotebookLM) with flexible drafting (Gems) in one workflow.
